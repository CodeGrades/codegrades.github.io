The CodeGrades Community Code of Conduct

The CodeGrades community is made up of members from around the globe with a diverse set of skills, personalities, and experiences. It is through these differences that our community experiences great successes and continued growth. When you're working with members of the community, we expect you to follow these guidelines which help steer our interactions and strive to keep CodeGrades a positive, successful, and growing community.

A member of the CodeGrades community is:

Open

Members of the community are open to collaboration, whether it's on code, projects, problems, or otherwise. We're receptive to constructive comment and criticism, as the experiences and skill sets of other members contribute to the whole of our efforts. We're accepting of all who wish to take part in our activities, fostering an environment where anyone can participate and everyone can make a difference.

Considerate

Members of the community are considerate of their peers -- other CodeGrades users, learners and mentors. We're thoughtful when addressing the efforts of others, keeping in mind that often times the labor was completed simply for the good of the community. We're attentive in our communications, whether in person or online, and we're tactful when approaching differing views.

Respectful

Members of the community are respectful. We're respectful of others, their positions, their skills, their commitments, and their efforts. We're respectful of the efforts of others that permeate the CodeGrades community. We're respectful of the processes set forth in the community, and we work within them. When we disagree, we are courteous in raising our issues.

Overall, we're good to each other. We contribute to this community not because we have to, but because we want to. If we remember that, these guidelines will come naturally.

Questions/comments/reports? Please write to hello@codegrades.com.

This code of conduct is based on the one developed by the Python Software Foundation for the Python community.
